Sara Sierschula | Non-Profit Professional | Software Programming Student

The career path to IT was accidental. After college I completed two years of AmeriCorps, then in 2004 I joined an upstart nonprofit focused on benefit access. I was fortunate to wear many hats at Benefits Data Trust BDT. I started off on the phones, helping low-income senior citizens in Philadelphia and across the state apply for benefit programs to help pay for the Medicare premiums as well as tax and rent rebates. I took on additional challenges over the years, including call center management and benefit policy research, before ending up on the software development team. As a subject matter expert on the intricacies of state and federal benefit programs, as well as BDT-specific work flows and processes, I crafted user stories for releases. Our web application tool allowed staff to give benefit estimations as well as help apply low-income individuals for benefit programs. Over the years we improved existing outreach programs as we built new benefits into the system. In my QA role I wrote specifications, added user stories to pivotal tracker, tested stories, communicated updates to staff, and supervised 2 QA project coordinators.

Throughout the past 5 years I've been involved in these non-technical aspects of software development. Now I'm ready to learn about programming! The projects above were part of a 12 week, part-time course called "Build Websites from Scratch" from Codecademy. The course primarily focused on HTML, CSS, Git and GitHub, Command Line, and Bootstrap, with a brief introduction to Javascript and JQuery at the end of the course. Each project focused on particular objectives. I'm proud of the work done so far, but know I have so much more to learn. I look forward to that challenge and taking this next step.
