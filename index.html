Sara Sierschula | Non-Profit Professional | Software Programming Student

My career path into IT has taken a non-profit route. After college I completed two years of AmeriCorps, then in 2004 I joined an upstart non-profit focused on public welfare benefit access. I was fortunate to wear many hats at Benefits Data Trust (BDT). I started off on the phones, helping low-income senior citizens in Philadelphia and across the state apply for benefit programs to help pay for Medicare premiums as well as tax and rent rebates. I took on additional challenges over the years, including call center management and benefit policy research, before ending up on the software development team. As a subject matter expert on the intricacies of state and federal benefit programs, as well as BDT-specific work flows and processes, I crafted user stories for software developers. The web application tool allowed staff to give benefit estimations for programs such as Medicaid and SNAP (aka Food Stamps) as well as help apply low-income individuals for these programs. Over the years we improved existing outreach programs as well as built new benefits into the system. In my QA role I wrote specifications, added user stories to pivotal tracker, tested stories, communicated updates to staff, and supervised two project test coordinators.

For many years now I've been involved in these non-technical aspects of software development. I'm now ready to learn about programming, to understand whatâ€™s under the hood. The projects above were part of a nine week, part-time course called "Build Websites from Scratch" from Codecademy. The course primarily focused on HTML, CSS, Git and GitHub, and Command Line, with a brief introduction to Bootstrap, Javascript, and JQuery at the end. Each project focused on particular objectives. The course focused on using Google Developer tools, so projects were not optimized for other browsers such as IE and Firefox. I'm proud of the work done so far, but know I have so much more to learn. I look forward to that challenge and taking this next step.
